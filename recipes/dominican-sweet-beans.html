Dominican Sweet Beans

HABICHUELAS CON DULCE

Have you ever tried Habichuelas con dulce (sweet beans)? Be prepared to be pleasantly surprised by this fantastic and uniquely Dominican sweet cream of beans. While a strange combination of dessert ingredients, Dominicans love this Lent tradition. And you may, too!

What is it?

Habichuelas con dulce is a Dominican dessert made with beans, milk, coconut milk, sweet potatoes (batata), raisins, and spices. The result is a soup-like sweet, creamy dessert that is served with our iconic galletas de leche, and toasted casabe. If you are curious about the history of this dessert, read this.

Ingredients

  • Boiled red kidney beans: 4 cups
  • Water 6 cups
  • Coconut milk 2 cups
  • Salt 1 teaspoon
  • Sugar (white, granulated) 1 cup
  • Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon
  • Cloves 10
  • Raisins 1/2 cup
  • Sweet potatoes 1/2lb, cut into small cubes

    • Garnish

  • Milk cookies 1 cup

Instructions

  1. Put the beans (and the water in which they boiled) in a blender and puree. Strain the beans to get rid of the skins and undissolved solids.
  2. Pour the beans, coconut milk, evaporated milk, salt, sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, cloves, and sweet potatoes into a pot. Simmer over very low heat until the sweet potatoes are cooked through. Stir regularly to avoid sticking. Add the raisins and simmer for another 10 minutes (don't worry that it may look too thin, the cream of beans will get much thicker when chilled). Remove the cinnamon sticks (and cloves, if you like).
  3. Remove from the heat and cool to room temperature. Chill before serving.
